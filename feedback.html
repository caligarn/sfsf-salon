Session 1 · Post-Event Survey · 2026

First Session
Feedback

From the Participants

After the first gathering of the SFSF Salon, we collected feedback from participants. Here's what they said — and what it means for the sessions ahead.

01 — Overall Reception

Strong positive response
to the first gathering.

Several participants described the event as energizing and intellectually rich. Many people appreciated the mix of disciplines and perspectives.

"Philosophers and engineers; niche literature readers and fans of pop culture; writers and readers."

The small size was widely seen as a strength. Participants liked the discussion format and the cap around ~20 people.

  • Multiple people suggested a more tight format with more time for breakout groups and smaller groups
  • Multiple people suggested the longer-term output of this group should be to create something
  • Multiple people wanted some reading material, so we will have a short story to read
02 — Group Composition & Community

The right people
in the right room.

The diversity of backgrounds was repeatedly mentioned as a core strength of the salon. People valued learning about each attendee's work and perspective.

There is interest in the salon becoming a nucleus for a larger science fiction community in San Francisco.

  • One suggestion was framing the broader mission as making San Francisco an epicenter for science fiction again

Some attendees are looking for collaboration opportunities.

"I am personally looking for some of my existing ideas to get shaped here, and maybe even team up with like-minded people."
03 — Structure & Facilitation

A little more
structure next time.

The most common feedback was the need for slightly more structure. Long open discussion sometimes allowed a few participants to dominate the conversation.

"I kinda felt like a captive audience while certain people rambled."

The event ran longer than expected, which some participants found tiring. The session scheduled for one hour went over two hours.

Suggestions
  • Clear time blocks for discussion
  • Light moderation to prevent "mic-holding"
  • A more defined beginning and ending to the structured portion
04 — Possible Format Improvements

Evolving the format.

Two-Phase Evening

Several attendees suggested splitting the evening into two phases:

  • Structured discussion around a topic or reading
  • Open social / mingle time afterward so people can leave without awkwardness
Book-Club Component

A book-club style component was suggested — shared reading or watching material beforehand.

Breakout Groups

Breakout groups were generally appreciated.

"The breakouts were great; the big group discussions can be a bit chaotic."
Topic Planning

Some people suggested planning topics ahead of time — vote on topics and dedicate future sessions to the selected themes.

05 — Creative Direction

Beyond discussion.
Into creation.

Multiple participants encouraged evolving the salon beyond discussion into creative collaboration.

"How do we make it a creative experience instead of just another sci-fi book/movie club?"
Workshops

Sessions where participants create something together.

Works-in-Progress

Sessions where people present works-in-progress and receive feedback.

Storyteller Sessions

Dedicated sessions helping a specific storyteller develop their project.

06 — Types of Sessions Proposed

A rotating format.

One attendee suggested rotating between three types of gatherings:

Broad Discussions

Open discussions about science fiction and culture.

Deep Dives

Highly focused deep dives into a single topic.

Story Development

Sessions centered on helping a specific storyteller develop a story or project.

07 — Long-Term Vision & Impact

What is the lasting impact?

Several people expressed curiosity about the long-term goal of the salon.

"What is the lasting impact you're aiming for?"
Testing Ground

Turning the group into a testing ground for science fiction ideas and prototypes.

Building Connections

Building connections between storytellers, technologists, and investors.

Supporting Projects

Supporting new science-fiction projects and funding pathways.

Influencing Stories

Influencing stories currently in development.

08 — Venue & Logistics

The location: perfect.

Venue and group size received strong positive feedback.

"The location: perfect."
"The number of people: excellent."
Suggestion

Experimenting with venues tied to themes — for example, selecting venues that match a topic like biotechnology or futurism.

The only logistical complaint mentioned was the general challenge of external locations and nightlife noise in San Francisco.

09 — Topics of Interest

What to explore next.

  • Funding and investment in science fiction
  • Bringing more science-fiction storytellers to San Francisco
  • Influencing current science fiction in production
  • Exploring intersections between technology, culture, and storytelling
11 — What's Next

Where we go from here.

Based on your feedback, here's the plan for the next sessions.

Session 2 — Suggested Reading

We'll have a short story to read before the session. Come prepared to speak briefly about it in the first 10 minutes — what resonated, what surprised you, what it got wrong.

Session 2 — Discussion

Sci-fi themes that we think are important to cover in this moment. What stories are resonating right now? What's missing from the conversation?

Session 3 — Breakout Groups

We'll break into small groups and talk about sci-fi ideas we would personally like to work on. This is where creation begins.

10 — Summary Sentiment

The right crowd for
something cool.

Participants consistently emphasized that the group already has the right ingredients.

High-Intelligence Crowd
Passionate Discussion
Cross-Disciplinary Mix
"You definitely have the right crowd for something cool."
